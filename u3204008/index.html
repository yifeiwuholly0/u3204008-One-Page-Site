17 October 2013 - 28 October 2013

New South Wales bushfires It was a devastating fire that swept through New South Wales. The firestarted because the average daily temperature in New South Wales was 3.4°C (6.1°F) above the historical average. High fuel loads,combined with warm, dry and windy weather, provided the dangerous conditions that fuelled the fires. At the peak of the fire season, there were over 100 fires across the state. The burned area reached 118,000 hectares, resulting in two deaths and two injuries and property damage totalling $94 million.

2 January 2015- 9 January 2015

Sampson Flat Bushfires
Continuing a sustained pattern of severe rainfall deficits over 27 months, rising temperatures and thunderstorms leading to lightning triggered a new spate of grass fires in South Australia, Australia. Property damage totalled $13 million, with over 20,000 hectares burnt, 0 deaths, and 134 injuries. Much native wildlife, livestock and household pets perished in the fires.

15 November 2015 - 26 November 2015

Esperance bushfires
This was a catastrophic forest fire caused by lightning that ignited the fire area in southern Western Australia. The total area destroyed was 310,000 hectares, four people died in the fire, and 19 houses were destroyed or severely damaged. It sparked discussion about the possibility of the critically endangered parrot becoming the first bird to become extinct in Western Australia for at least 200 years.

25 November 2015 - 2 December 2015

Pinery bushfire
A devastating fire swept through South Australia and triggered a dust storm that affected north-central South Australia and the Barossa Valley towns. Two people lost their lives, and 90 were injured in the fire and completely burned 388 non- residential structures, 93 pieces of farm equipment and 98 other vehicles. Property damage totalled $1.7 million, and over 85,000 hectares were burnt.

5 January to 22 January 2016

Murray Road bushfire
Bushfires are burning in western Australia. The origin is that rainfall in the region is far below average. A lightning strike in Lane Poole Reserve, a state forest in Jarrah and Marri, sparked two fires in the Lane Poole Reserve State Forest, burning near Waroona. About 70,000 hectares of land were burned, killing two people and injuring five others. One hundred eighty-one buildings were burned.

Australian bushfire season (Black Summer)
The Australian bushfires throughout Australia originated from fires started during the Australian bushfire season, lightning strikes, accidents, suspected arson, global warming, positive Indian Ocean dipole, large fuel loads. Drought and high temperatures. The total area destroyed is about 18,636,079 hectares. 34 people died, about 2,600 houses and property were lost (at least 1 billion), wild animals are estimated to have died, and some species are considered to be extinct.

August 2020 - June 2021

Australian BushfireSeasons
Bushfire seasons occur in New South Wales, Northern Territory, Queensland, South Australia and Western Australia. The bushfire outlook forecasts an average fire potential in Queensland with good grass growth in many areas leading to an increased risk of grass fires and above-normal season in the Kimberley region of Western Australia due to good rainfall from tropical cyclones, a regular but earlier season in the Northern Territory, an above-normal season on the southern coast of New South Wales and a regular-season elsewhere. The total area destroyed was 250,000 hectares.
